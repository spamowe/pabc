Membership Information

Membership in the Club is open to all individuals,businesses and public and private organizations, who are interested or engaged in business and/or professional services. Membership can be in the name of an individual, or business enterprise or professional service.

Membership Categories

Founder - This category is reserved for founding members

Individual - Recommendation of two members and an annual fee required

Business - Recommendation of two members and an annual fee required, limit one person

Honorary - No fee

Joining the club will assist the new member in expanding his/her business through meeting new clients or customers and developing new contacts.

  • Supports its members' business initiatives.
  • Sponsors networking opportunities for its members.
  • Facilitates access to business resources.
  • Disseminates business information.
  • Offers educational opportunities.
  • Assists American and Polish students in finding practical training opportunities.
  • Partners with other organizations.
  • Facilitates international business exchange.
  • Sponsors research related to the Polish-American Business Community.
  • Promotes continuous improvement in bilateral relations between the United States and Poland.
  • Recognizes individuals for the outstanding activities in the area of the Polish-American affairs.

About Us

The Polish-American Business Club, Inc. was created in response to a need for a Polish-American business organization in the Greater New York area representing both businessmen and professionals of Polish descent from the States of New York and New Jersey.

The organization was expected to represent and promote the best values and traditions of the Polish-American business community and support the outstanding activities of its members.

The Meeting of Founders, held on February 25, 2000 at Lanidex Plaza, Parsippany, New Jersey, in the office of Stebco, Inc. gathered 14 representatives of the Polish-American business community from New York and New Jersey. The meeting concluded with the resolution on creation of the Polish-American Business Club (PABC) with the following eight founding members: Adam Bak, Jerzy Lesniak, Marek Lyzwinski, Krzysztof Matyszczyk, Krzysztof Plocienniczak, Henryk Rozek, Slawomir Tomkowski and Ludwik Wnekowicz.

Two individuals, who were not present at the Meeting of Founders and who contributed to the creation of the Club were recognized as Honorary Founding Members Jerzy Kleniewski, Consular Officer at the Consulate General of the Republic of Poland and Andrzej Maria Laganowski, Trade Commissioner of the Economic Section of the Embassy of Poland, Office in New York (during the year 2000).

The Club was incorporated on July 5, 2000 at Albany, New York as a Type A Corporation defined in section 201 of the Not-for-Profit Corporation Law. Since that time, the Club and its members have been active promoting Polish-American business and professionals in the United States and Poland when contributing to development of "new bridges" between the two nations.

Our Objective
Polish-American Business Club's mission is to promote the Polish-American Business and Professional Community in North America and Poland.

Eligibility
Membership in the Club is open to all individuals, businesses and public and private organizations, who are interested or engaged in business and/or professional services. Membership can be in the name of an individual, or business enterprise or professional service.
